Why a paper wallet?

Paper wallets are a fast and convenient way to store Eurocoin (EURO) offline. You can even spend or gift Eurocoins without ever connecting to the internet by treating the wallets as cash. Paper wallets are a physical way to store and spend Eurocoin (EURO), like a 20€ bill is a physical way to store and spend 20€. Like a 20€ bill, paper wallets can be lost or stolen. Store your paper wallet in a safe location where only you can access.

Extra Security Tips

Our paper wallet is generated using a browser-side random address generator. This means the wallet is created in your browser. No data is transmitted to our servers or across the web. Generally, it is acceptably safe to use as is. However, if you are using the wallet for larger amounts or just want to ensure extra security please take these tips into consideration.

Run Offline

Running the generator off a live site presents bad actors the potential to intercept your paper wallet's private key. It's not likely, but to mitigate the risk we highly recommend downloading the page files from our Github first. Then turn off your internet connection and run the page locally. Just open the downloaded zip and open the index.html file in your browser. Download Generator

Clean Operating System

Make sure you are using a trusted computer free from any viruses or malware. Do not create your wallet in view of any prying eyes or cameras.

Safe Printer

Again make sure you are using a trusted printer. Do not wirelessly connect with the printer. Use a direct connection instead, and make sure no data is saved in your print queue or on the printer afterwards.

Don't Save

Do not save your paper wallet as an image or digital file. Paper cannot be hacked, your computer can be.

Treat it like Cash

Once your wallet is made and you have sent funds to it, it is essentially like cash. Whoever physically holds it can access the funds. It can be lost, stolen, or destroyed and there is no backup. Take whatever precautions necessary to ensure it stays safe until needed.

How to Use Our Paper Wallet

How do I keep it safe?

You don’t want anyone to access your Eurocoins, that’s why you have to keep your paper wallet in a safe place. You should choose a place in your home that you exclusively have access to. This can be a safe deposit box, a hidden place in your room, a cabinet, or even a specific page of your favorite book. It's good practice to keep your paper wallet in a watertight bag (e.g. Zip-Loc) or a box where water or other liquids can’t damage it.

How do I load funds to a paper wallet?

First, you need a “normal” Eurocoin wallet (either an online, exchange or software wallet), which you will use to load your paper wallet. After you have the desired amount of Eurocoin (EURO) in your “normal” wallet, you can deposit funds to the paper wallet by scanning the wallet’s public address and sending Eurocoin (EURO) there.

How do I verify how much is on it?

You can verify your Eurocoin (EURO) balance by using a block explorer and scanning the public address on your paper wallet (we recommend using your phone’s camera for that). You can also verify your balance by typing in your public key to the search bar.

How do I withdraw funds?

To withdraw funds from your paper wallet, install a digital wallet and scan the private key inside the application. For security reasons, if you withdraw funds from your paper wallet, you should withdraw ALL of your balance at once. Don’t leave any funds in your paper wallet, as it could get lost. NEVER use the same paper wallet to deposit funds again! Create a new one instead.
